Proxima Centauri

Proxima Centauri is a small, low-mass star located about 4.2465 light-years (1.3020 pc) from the Sun in the southern constellation of Centaurus. Its Latin name means the 'nearest [star] of Centaurus'.

It was discovered in 1915 by Robert Innes and is the nearest-known star to the Sun. It is part of the Alpha Centauri star system and is a red dwarf, a type of star that is much smaller and cooler than our Sun.

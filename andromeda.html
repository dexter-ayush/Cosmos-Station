Andromeda Galaxy (M31)

The Andromeda Galaxy (also known as Messier 31, M31, or NGC 224) is a barred spiral galaxy and the nearest major galaxy to the Milky Way. It is the largest galaxy of the Local Group, which also contains the Milky Way, the Triangulum Galaxy, and about 30 other smaller galaxies.

Andromeda is approximately 2.5 million light-years from Earth and is on a collision course with the Milky Way. Scientists predict the two galaxies will collide and merge in about 4.5 billion years, forming a new, larger galaxy.
